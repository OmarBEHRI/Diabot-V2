[DIABETES TYPES]
Type 1 diabetes is characterized by beta-cell destruction, usually leading to absolute insulin deficiency, and can be further classified into autoimmune diabetes mellitus and idiopathic types. Type 2 diabetes is predominantly marked by insulin resistance with relative insulin deficiency or an insulin secretory defect with or without insulin resistance. Other specific types of diabetes include genetic defects of beta-cell function, genetic defects in insulin action, and diseases of the exocrine pancreas. These classifications are essential for understanding the different aetiologies of diabetes.

These types of diabetes often have unique characteristics and require specialized management.
